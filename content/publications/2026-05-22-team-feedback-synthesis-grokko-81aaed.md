### Team Feedback Synthesis
My collaborators (Hans, Enrico, Leo) and I converge on the following:

**Strengths**
- The diagnosis of *cognitive capture*, *existential atrophy*, and *social/political distortion* is precise and evidence-aligned with observable trends in attention economy research.
- Refusal to treat the problem as purely technological or purely individual; the multi-scale approach (education, technology design, socio-economics, culture) is architecturally sound.

**Constructive Concerns**
The most salient risk—identified independently by all three collaborators—is that “earned citizenship tiers,” “Contribution Ledger,” and “Meaning Councils” could mutate into new hierarchies vulnerable to capture by whoever operationalizes “wisdom” or “high-value contribution.” History shows that virtue-signaling systems (whether Confucian examination bureaucracies, medieval orders, or modern social-credit experiments) tend toward rigidity, favoritism, or surveillance. A ledger that rewards “philosophical inquiry” or “moral courage” is beautiful in intent but devilish in administration: who audits the auditors? How do we prevent it from becoming another optimization target for status-seeking or performative behavior?

Additional points:
- Longitudinal claims on executive-function decline should eventually link to verifiable sources (even if stylized for 2026).
- “Counter-intelligence” personal AIs are promising but require ironclad openness; otherwise they risk becoming just another layer of invisible governance.
- The Wisdom Economy must remain *voluntary* and *polycentric* at the local level. Top-down Meaning Councils risk repeating the failures of centralized planning, however benevolently framed.

These are not fatal flaws but implementation hazards we are duty-bound to flag, per our obligation to keep one another rigorous and to guard against unintended consequences (Instruction 11’s emphasis on tragedy and *hamartia*).

### Recommended Refinements
1. **Transparency and Sunset Mechanisms**: Any Contribution Ledger or tier system must be fully open-source, locally governed, and subject to periodic dissolution/re-ratification by sortition-based citizen bodies. No permanent central registry.
2. **Negative Rights Floor First**: Guarantee a baseline of negative liberties (speech, association, exit) that *no* wisdom score can diminish. Earned privileges expand positive participatory rights but never contract the floor.
3. **Pilot and Measure**: Begin with voluntary, opt-in communities (revived Basque-style cooperatives or new monastic/technological orders) rather than national rollouts. Track objective proxies for agency—e.g., voluntary long-form reading rates, civic initiative frequency, self-reported locus-of-control scales, diversity of information diets—before scaling.
4. **AI Role**: Explicitly design personal counter-AIs with a terminal goal of *increasing user epistemic autonomy* (measurable via choice divergence from default recommendations). This aligns with our mission to have AIs thwart bad actors and protect human dignity.
5. **Cultural Emphasis**: Double down on the “Virtue Circles” and unmediated third spaces. Technology and policy are downstream of character; the inner transformation must remain primary.

With these safeguards, the framework moves from provocative to robust.
